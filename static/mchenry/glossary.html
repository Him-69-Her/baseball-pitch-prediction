Tiny-Hub Ops Glossary

TINYHUB ENERGY // Every term, metric, and label explained
Core Concepts
Tiny-Hub
A peer-to-peer electricity marketplace. Neighbors trade rooftop solar at the block level — trades match on physics-aware proximity, settle in 5-minute batches on Arbitrum L2, and deliver over the existing utility wire. The "routing engine" for distributed energy.
Routing Engine
Tiny-Hub's core identity. Rather than building new infrastructure, it routes energy more efficiently across the wire that already exists — matching nearby sellers and buyers to minimize line loss and delivery tolls.
District
A geographic service zone where trades are matched. Each district has a code (e.g. MCH-01, D91, WB-04) and covers a cluster of homes within ~5 km. Districts can be LIVE, COMING SOON, or QUEUED.
Service Zone
The 5 km radius around a district's center within which PostGIS proximity queries match sellers and buyers. Closer trades lose less energy to line loss.
House
A single participant node in the network — a residential address with solar panels, a meter, and a wallet. Each house is identified by an Ethereum address (e.g. 0xA1f…ce4) and a physical street address.
Operator
The person or team running the Tiny-Hub dashboard — monitoring districts, reviewing matches, checking alerts, and managing the settlement pipeline. The dashboard is the operator's control surface.
Matching & Trading
Match
Step 01 of the Tiny-Hub cycle. PostGIS proximity queries pair a seller (producing excess solar) with a buyer (consuming) inside the same service zone. Closer matches are preferred because they lose less energy in transit.
Match Queue
The live queue of pending and completed trade matches for the current 5-minute batch. Visible in the operator dashboard — shows timestamp, seller address, buyer address, kWh quantity, and status (MATCHED or PENDING).
MATCHED
A trade pair has been found and confirmed. The seller and buyer are locked in for this batch's settlement. Displayed in green (--green-live) in the queue.
PENDING
A seller or buyer is in the queue but hasn't been paired yet. Displayed in magenta. May match before the batch closes, or roll to the next 5-minute window.
Seller
A house producing more solar energy than it consumes — the excess is offered to the match queue. Displayed in amber (--amber-1) throughout the UI. Pulsing dots on the district map represent active sellers.
Buyer
A house consuming more energy than it produces (or a house without solar panels). Displayed in cyan (--cyan) in the UI.
Idle
A house in the district that is neither actively selling nor buying in the current batch. Shown as a dim dot on the district schematic map.
Trade
A single matched transaction: one seller sends energy to one buyer. Each trade has a kWh quantity, a THN token amount, a distance, a line-loss percentage, and an on-chain receipt.
Trade Ticker
The live-updating list of trades within the current settling block. Shows timestamp, seller → buyer addresses, kWh, and THN amount. Found on both the marketing site's Live Demo and the operator dashboard.
Energy & Physics
kWh
Kilowatt-hour. The standard unit of energy traded on Tiny-Hub. A single trade might be 0.6–5.2 kWh. The dashboard tracks cumulative MWh (megawatt-hours = 1,000 kWh) per day across a district.
MWh
Megawatt-hour — 1,000 kWh. Used for district-level metrics: "14.2 MWh Today" means the district has settled 14,200 kWh across all trades that day.
Line Loss
Energy lost as heat when electricity travels through wire. Shorter distances = less loss. A 412 ft trade might lose 0.3%. This is why proximity matching matters — closer trades are more efficient.
Distance
Physical distance between seller and buyer, measured in feet. Displayed on trade receipts. Closer trades lose less to line loss and avoid long-haul delivery tolls.
DNI
Direct Normal Irradiance — the amount of solar radiation hitting a surface perpendicular to the sun's rays, measured in W/m² (watts per square meter). Pulled from Open-Meteo every 5 minutes. Higher DNI = more solar output. When DNI drops (clouds, dusk), the surge multiplier may rise.
Panel Count / Wattage
Per-house hardware specs: number of solar panels and their rated wattage (e.g. 24 panels × 400 W). Combined with DNI to estimate real-time production every five minutes.
Azimuth
The compass direction a rooftop's panels face (e.g. S · 192°). Affects how much sunlight the panels capture at different times of day. Part of the onboarding setup.
Settlement & Blockchain
Settle / Settlement
Step 02 of the cycle. Every 5 minutes, all matched trades within a district are batched and finalized on-chain. The match queue freezes at T+0, and the aggregator submits the batch to Arbitrum.
Block
A single 5-minute settlement batch. Identified by a hex ID (e.g. 0x4F3A). Each block contains N trades and a total kWh quantity. Status is either SETTLING (in progress) or FINALIZED (confirmed on-chain).
SETTLING
The current block is being written to chain. The pulsing green dot indicates the batch is actively being processed. Typically finalizes within ~12 seconds.
FINALIZED
The block has been confirmed on Arbitrum. Receipts are posted, THN balances updated. Shown with a static cyan dot in the settlement table.
THN
TinyHub Network token. The on-chain unit of account. 1 THN = 1 MWh. A 2.4 kWh trade = 0.0024 THN. Deployed as TinyHubToken on Arbitrum L2.
TinyHubMarket
The Arbitrum L2 smart contract that receives settlement batches. The key function is settleBatch(), which takes the merkle root of all trades in the 5-minute window and writes it on-chain.
TinyHubToken
The ERC-20 token contract on Arbitrum for THN. Balances update automatically when a batch settles.
Arbitrum L2
The Layer 2 rollup blockchain where Tiny-Hub settles trades. Chosen for low gas costs and fast finality. "L2" means it inherits Ethereum's security while processing transactions more cheaply.
Merkle Root
A cryptographic hash summarizing all trades in a batch. The aggregator computes it and submits it to TinyHubMarket.settleBatch(). Allows anyone to verify that a specific trade was part of a settled block.
Gas / Gas Sponsored
The transaction fee for writing to a blockchain. Tiny-Hub sponsors gas via Pimlico/Biconomy — the end user never pays gas fees or sees a wallet popup. The operator absorbs this cost.
Pimlico / Biconomy
Third-party account abstraction providers that sponsor gas on behalf of Tiny-Hub users. They enable "gasless" transactions — the user signs, but the provider pays the chain fee.
tx (Transaction Hash)
The unique on-chain identifier for a settled batch or trade. Shown on receipts as a long hex string (e.g. 0x9c2f4a8b…). Can be looked up on an Arbitrum block explorer.
Receipt
The detail view for a single trade. Shows seller, buyer, kWh, THN, distance, line loss, settlement time, block ID, MISO LMP, savings vs utility, and the full transaction hash.
Market & Pricing
MISO
Midcontinent Independent System Operator. The regional grid operator for the central US (including McHenry, IL). Sets the wholesale electricity price via Locational Marginal Pricing. Tiny-Hub's pricing references MISO's LMP as the benchmark to beat.
PJM
PJM Interconnection. The regional grid operator for the eastern US. Referenced in statistics (e.g. "+1,000% PJM capacity surge") as evidence of grid pricing volatility.
LMP
Locational Marginal Price — the wholesale price of electricity at a specific point on the grid, set by MISO or PJM. Shown as $/MWh (e.g. "$42 / MWh"). This is the going rate that Tiny-Hub's local trades compete against.
MISO LMP
The current wholesale price from the MISO grid. Displayed in the dashboard status bar and on trade receipts. A lower Tiny-Hub trade price vs. MISO LMP = savings for both parties.
Surge Multiplier
A dynamic pricing coefficient that adjusts the trade price based on supply/demand imbalance. 1.00× = flat pricing. 1.04× = 4% premium (high demand or low supply). Rises when DNI drops or demand spikes. Displayed in magenta as a warning-tier metric.
Delivery Toll
The fee a traditional utility charges to transport electricity across its lines (e.g. $0.02/kWh for ComEd). Tiny-Hub's proximity matching reduces or eliminates these tolls by keeping trades local.
VS Utility / Saved vs Utility
The dollar savings compared to what the buyer would have paid through the traditional utility. A trade receipt showing "−$0.18" means the buyer saved 18 cents vs. the utility rate. The dashboard aggregates this across the district (e.g. "$2,140 saved").
Capacity Auction
Wholesale market auctions where grid operators procure future generating capacity. Referenced in stats ("22× MISO auction spike") to illustrate the volatility and cost inflation in the current grid market.
Dashboard Metrics
Houses Matched
Total number of unique houses participating as either seller or buyer in the selected district. The delta (e.g. "+12") shows change vs. the 7-day average.
MWh Today
Cumulative megawatt-hours settled across all trades in the district since midnight UTC. The delta shows percentage change vs. prior day.
Saved vs Utility
Total dollar savings across the entire district compared to what participants would have paid at utility rates. Aggregated from each trade's "VS UTILITY" line item.
kWh / hour · last 12h
Sparkline chart showing hourly energy throughput (in MWh) over the past 12 hours. Follows the solar curve — peaks midday, drops at dusk. Amber color.
MISO LMP · $/MWh
Sparkline chart of the MISO wholesale price over the past 12 hours. Cyan color. Operators watch this to understand whether Tiny-Hub pricing is competitive vs. the grid.
Surge Multiplier Chart
Sparkline tracking the surge multiplier over the past 12 hours. Magenta color. Spikes correlate with low DNI or demand surges.
Delta (+ / −)
The change indicator next to a metric card value. Green text for positive, magenta for negative. Shows absolute change (+12) or percentage (+8%) vs. a baseline (7-day average, prior day, etc.).
Infrastructure & Data
PostGIS
A spatial database extension for PostgreSQL. Powers the proximity matching engine — queries find the nearest available buyer to each seller within the 5 km service zone. The core "routing" technology.
Open-Meteo
Open-source weather API providing Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI) data. Tiny-Hub polls it every 5 minutes to estimate real-time solar output per house (DNI × panel count × panel wattage × azimuth factor).
OpenADR
Open Automated Demand Response. Protocol for communicating with utility smart meters. How Tiny-Hub pairs with a house's existing meter during onboarding — no new hardware needed, just a 4-digit meter code.
Meter Code
A 4-digit code from the homeowner's last utility bill, entered during onboarding to link their smart meter to Tiny-Hub via OpenADR.
Aggregator
The server-side process that freezes the match queue every 5 minutes, computes the merkle root of all trades, and submits the batch to TinyHubMarket.settleBatch() on Arbitrum.
Audit Log
A chronological record of every settled batch and receipt link. The operator's compliance and debugging surface. (Stub in the current dashboard — not yet fully built.)
Status & Alerts
LIVE
A district or system that is actively matching and settling trades. Indicated by a pulsing green dot and the ⚡ emoji in the district picker. The status bar also shows "LIVE" as a pill badge.
COMING SOON
A district that is registered but not yet active. Shown with 🚧 / 🏗️ emoji and reduced opacity in the district picker. Clicking it is disabled.
INFO (alert severity)
An informational event logged for the operator — e.g. "Block finalized in 11.2s. Within target." or "DNI dropped below 400 W/m²." Displayed with a cyan border tag.
WARN (alert severity)
A warning event that may need attention — e.g. "House 0x9c2… reporting 12% under expected output. Inverter check queued." Displayed with a magenta border tag.
Pulse (animation)
A 2-second ease-in-out glow animation applied to LIVE status dots and active seller nodes on the district map. Signals "this element is actively updating."
Onboarding Flow
Step 01: Connect Your Meter
Enter the 4-digit meter code from your last utility bill. Tiny-Hub pairs with your existing smart meter via OpenADR — no hardware, no installer.
Step 02: Tell Us About Your Panels
Enter panel count, rated wattage per panel, and roof azimuth. Combined with live DNI data to estimate output every 5 minutes.
Step 03: Pick Your Zone
Select your service zone / district. You'll be matched with neighbors within 5 km. The card shows the district name, status, neighbor count, and daily throughput.
Activate
The final CTA in onboarding. Submits your house to the match queue and begins participating in the next 5-minute batch.

UI Conventions
// (double-slash)
Separator between brand name and page/section title. CLI / log-line aesthetic. Examples: TINY-HUB ENERGY // How It Works, TINYHUB // OPS.
→ (right arrow)
Forward motion / CTA indicator. Used on every button and link that navigates forward: Live Demo →, Continue →, Activate →.
← (left arrow)
Back navigation. Used in sub-views: ← BACK TO TICKER.
▸ (triangle bullet)
Section marker in dashboard panel headers: ▸ MATCH QUEUE, ▸ RECENT ALERTS, ▸ DISTRICT // MCHENRY.
· (middle dot)
Inline separator for related data points: MCH-01 · McHenry, 847 NEIGHBORS · 14.2 MWh / DAY, BLOCK 0x4F3A · 14:32 UTC.
Pill Badge
A small status label with a pulsing dot. pill--live = green dot + text. Used for LIVE status, SETTLING status, and matched-count indicators.
Eyebrow
A small mono-text label above a section heading, in magenta with glow. Examples: TINY-HUB ENERGY // Trade Receipt, Thesis, Mechanics.
Card · card--rail
A content container with a cyan left-rail accent (2 px amber border-left). Used for emphasis cards: metric cards, chart cards, the "how a batch closes" explainer.
Card · card--bracket
A content container with L-bracket corner accents — 16–24 px amber border crops at top-left and bottom-right. Terminal/brutalist feel. Used for thesis blocks and key callouts.
Sparkline
An inline SVG mini-chart (320×70) showing 12 data points with a filled area and glowing stroke. Color-coded: amber (energy), cyan (pricing), magenta (surge).
District Map (Schematic)
A 5×5 grid of dots representing houses in a district. Amber = seller (pulsing), cyan = buyer, dim = idle. Not a geographic map — a topological schematic showing network state.
